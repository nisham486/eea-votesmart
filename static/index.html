First-Time Voter Guide 🗺

Everything you need to cast your first ballot with confidence.

1

Check Your Eligibility

U.S. citizen, at least 18 on Election Day, meet residency requirements. Felony rules vary by state.

✓ First-time voter essential
2

Register to Vote

Register at vote.gov online, by mail, or in person at your DMV. Deadline is usually 15–30 days before election day.

✓ Required in most states
3

Understand Your Ballot

Research candidates and ballot measures. Voter guides are published by your local election office and nonprofits.

✓ Prepare in advance
4

Choose How to Vote

In-person on Election Day, early voting (1–2 weeks before), or by mail/absentee. Rules vary by state.

✓ Multiple options available
5

Know What to Bring

ID requirements vary by state. Check your state's requirements at vote.gov before heading to the polls.

✓ ID laws vary by state
6

Cast Your Ballot

Polls are typically open 6 AM–8 PM. If you're in line when polls close, you have the right to vote.

✓ Your vote is your voice
7

After Election Day

Track your mail ballot online. For problems, call Election Protection: 1-866-OUR-VOTE.

✓ Track your vote
